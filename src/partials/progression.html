Progression

From single pulse to layered rhythm.

Early levels introduce one clear wave pattern with generous timing gaps. You learn to sense the interval rather than react blindly.
Later stages introduce layered pulses with offset rhythms. Safe windows shrink. Overlapping waves demand anticipation instead of instinct.

In advanced levels, tempo accelerates and distortion consequences become sharper. The silence between pulses becomes thinner — and more valuable.

After a while, you'll find yourself knowing when those quiet spots are coming before they actually hit.
